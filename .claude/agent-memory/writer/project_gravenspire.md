## Six Factions (voice summary from art-bible §7.7 + §4.2)

- **Vampire Court:** Formal chancery hand; gray-blue vellum; third-person institutional; "sustained formality, a social order maintained long past the conditions that created it." Square wax seal. Arrives rolled. Not menace — preservation perfected past reason.
- **Ghoul Syndicate:** Cramped operational cipher on oily parchment; corrections struck through; iron die stamp (tally-mark motif); first-person plural; terse; abbreviated; warm interior palette (Old-wax ochre) but operationally dangerous. "Working material, not statement material."
- **Living Resistance:** Rough linen paper; multiple hands; practical; no seal; no formality; trade-district ochre palette; "the city's former baseline." Commercial envelopes repurposed. Pragmatism, not protocol.
- **Necromancer Academy:** Academic parchment; humanistic minuscule; most legible hand; systematic marginal annotation; scholarly, warm-intellectual.
- **Cult of the Pale King:** Backward-leaning devotional script; rough unbleached linen; no seal (stone-impression only); no pins — documents placed not posted.
- **Haunt Collective:** No consistent stock, no consistent hand, no seal; defaced prior seals; improvised mounting.

## Style rules for all faction writing
- No em-dashes used stylistically
- No true-black / true-white references; stay in material-warm vocabulary (stone, candle, wax, pine-tar, iron, linen, parchment)
- No RPG vocabulary (quest, XP, stats, mob, dungeon, loot)
- No tech-industry vocabulary in faction pieces
- No marketing verbs

## Devlog voice
Robin Sloan newsletter register; Ursula K. Le Guin craft-book tone. Serious, earnest, specific. No ironic detachment. No "hey everyone" openings. Light craft-book vocabulary permitted; avoid startup jargon.
